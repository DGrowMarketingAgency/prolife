POSSIBLE CAUSE MAY INCLUDE:

Hormonal: Disturbances of hormonal secretion in the body can cause an abnormal build-up of the lining of the uterus, which then breaks down leading to heavy menstrual loss. Common examples include PCOS or thyroid disorders.

Uterine Fibroids: Non-cancerous growths that develop in or around the uterus, which can cause heavy bleeding and pelvic pain.

Uterine / Endometrial Polyps: Small, soft growths on the inner wall of the uterus that can lead to irregular or heavy menstrual bleeding.

Adenomyosis: A condition where the inner lining of the uterus grows into the uterine wall, causing painful, heavy periods and cramping.

Rare Causes: Include clotting disorders, certain medications, and endometrial cancer, which may require further evaluation.

INVESTIGATIONS MAY INCLUDE:

Pelvic Examination: Used to assess the size of the uterus. Pap smear and swabs may be done at the same time to rule out infections or abnormalities.

Blood Tests: Help check for anaemia, thyroid problems, or hormonal imbalances that could contribute to abnormal bleeding.

Pelvic Ultrasound: Imaging test to check for fibroids, cysts, or structural abnormalities in the uterus and ovaries.

Endometrial Biopsy: A small tissue sample is taken from the lining of the uterus to check for abnormal cells or infection.

Hysteroscopy and Curettage: Allows direct visualization of the uterine cavity with a small camera to remove or sample tissue if needed.
