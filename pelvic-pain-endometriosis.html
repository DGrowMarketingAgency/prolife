Pelvic Pain & Endometriosis

What is Pelvic Pain & Endometriosis?

Pelvic Pain / Chronic Pelvic Pain is ongoing pain in the lower abdomen or pelvic. It is very common, affecting about 1 in 6 women. Painful periods, medically termed dysmenorrhea, are another common problem, impacting up to 1 in 2 menstruating women and significantly affecting their quality of life. Additionally, endometriosis, a prevalent condition, involves the growth of tissue similar to the endometrium outside the uterus.

What are the causes of Pelvic Pain & Endometriosis?

Pelvic pain can arise from various sources, including menstrual cramps, ovarian cysts, pelvic inflammatory disease (PID), irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), urinary tract infections (UTIs), and fibroids.

However, endometriosis, a gynecological condition where the endometrial tissue grows outside the uterus, is a significant cause of chronic pelvic pain. The exact reasons for endometriosis remain unclear, but theories suggest retrograde menstruation, immune system disorders, hormonal imbalances, and genetic predisposition as potential factors. Understanding these causes is crucial for early diagnosis and appropriate management of pelvic pain and endometriosis, ensuring improved quality of life for affected individuals.

Treatment Procedure for Pelvic Pain & Endometriosis

At our facility, we empathize with the challenges of dealing with pelvic pain and offer comprehensive assistance in identifying its root cause. Our approach includes a thorough assessment encompassing a detailed evaluation of your symptoms, a physical examination, and pelvic ultrasound imaging. In certain cases, a laparoscopy, an abdominal and pelvic cavity examination using a laparoscope, may be performed to gain further insights.
